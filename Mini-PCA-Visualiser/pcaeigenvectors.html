Instructions for use

Here we transform the data onto a lower k dimensional subspace. The axes are the first k principal components, selected by taking the first k eigenvectors of the covariance matrix as ordered by descending associated eigenvalue.

Move the slider to determine the number of principal components used (dimensions of the subspace). THe transformation matrix is the augmented matrix consisting of the eigenvectors of the covariance matrix. We multiply this by the normalised data matrix.

For 2 principal components we do the following $$ \small \begin{bmatrix} -0.90 & 1.03 & -1.34 & -1.31 \\ -1.14 & -0.12 & -1.33 & -1.30 \\ -1.38 & 0.34 & -1.40 & -1.31 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots \\ \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ \small \cdot \begin{bmatrix} -0.52 & -0.37 \\ 0.26 & -0.93 \\ -0.57 & -0.07 \\ -0.52 & -0.37 \\ \end{bmatrix} $$
