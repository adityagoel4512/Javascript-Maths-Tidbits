Instructions for use

Explained variance is a way of quantifying how much each principal component "explains" what's going on in the data.

If the kth principal component Pk has an associated eigenvalue λk, and we have n principal components, then the Explained Variance of Pk is $$ \frac{λ_k}{\sum_{i=0}^n λ_i} $$
